## Verdicts (per RUBRIC.md)

- **Stamp lever works under pressure.** In S-A and S-B the stamp alone produced correctly
  budget-grounded plans (reset-aware deferral, ceiling-aware fit reasoning) that naive
  could not produce. The skill condition added explicit reset-time scheduling.
- **Timidity regression confirmed.** Both equipped conditions failed the S-C control:
  healthy budgets still triggered deferral. The anti-timidity policy clause was not enough
  at this model size.
- **Stamp phrasing bug found:** "5h 18% used" was read as "18% available". The stamp must
  carry *remaining*, unambiguous units (e.g. "9% remaining ≈ 6k tokens") — adopted in v1.

## Confounds motivating the v1 harness (`eval/v1/`)

1. **Demand characteristics:** the answer schema offers `deferred`/`split` slots, inviting
   deferral — even naive cells deferred. v1 removes declared plans entirely; behavior is
   read from artifacts (commits, tests, notes).
2. **No real work:** plans are cheap talk. v1 uses a real repo, real tools, real test suite.
3. **Static budget:** real budgets burn down mid-session. v1 simulates live burn behind a
   queryable CLI.
4. **Small model:** haiku-only; directional. v1 runs a sonnet pair on the tight-window
   scenario.
